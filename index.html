What are Pueblos Mágicos?

The Pueblo Mágico program was created to popularize cities and towns which have ‘magical’ attributes. Towns are selected if they can show that they possess culturally significant events, stories, and legends. The program aims to show that the culture in Mexico goes much deeper than what appears on the surface, in the form of local mythology, local history, and family traditions. These values which may not be found in other places in Mexico are what makes them so special (Clausen, 321).

The Beginning

The first Pueblos Mágicos were established in 2001. The Ministry of Tourism elected 30 towns to participate in the program as a pilot program. This bolstered their historical attributes and allowed an amplification of their culture. The result being an improvement in how they are viewed by surrounding populations as well as visitors (Estudios Sociales 222).

Their Impact

Since the program’s initiation in 2001, over 80 towns have been accepted and enrolled. The majority of these towns were elected due to their indigenous origins or colonial legacy. Some towns were enrolled in order to preserve their traditional organizational principles. A select few have significant historical events that bring meaning to the visitors (322).
